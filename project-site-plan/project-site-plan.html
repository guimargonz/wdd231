Site Plan: Urban Myco-Forager's Companion

1. Site Name

Name: Urban Myco-Forager's Companion

Reason: The name clearly indicates the target audience (urban foragers), the subject (mycology/mushrooms), and the site's role (a helpful guide or companion).

Optional Domain Idea: `urbanmyco.guide`

2. Site Purpose

The primary purpose of the "Urban Myco-Forager's Companion" website is to provide accessible, clear, and reliable information for safely identifying common edible and notable (non-edible/toxic) mushrooms found in urban and suburban green spaces. The site aims to empower enthusiasts with knowledge on key identification features, seasonality, and critical safety warnings, including information on dangerous look-alikes. It will also offer resources for further learning and promote an ethical, respectful approach to foraging within community environments. Ultimately, the site serves as an introductory educational hub for aspiring urban mycologists.

3. Scenarios

  • "I found a mushroom in the local park after a rain shower. How can I tell if it's one of the common edible types for this area or something I should avoid?"
  • "What are some key visual differences between the edible 'Shaggy Mane' and potentially harmful look-alikes I might encounter on a city trail?"
  • "Are there any specific safety precautions or ethical guidelines I should be aware of before I consider picking mushrooms in public urban spaces?"

4. Color Schema

Primary: Deep Forest Green
#2E4628

Secondary: Earthy Brown
#6B4F40

Accent: Golden Ochre
#D4A017

Background (Light): Light Beige
#F5F5DC

Text (Dark): Dark Grey
#333333

Usage:

  • Deep Forest Green (#2E4628): Main headings (H1, H2), primary call-to-action button backgrounds, footer background, key section borders or accents.
  • Earthy Brown (#6B4F40): Subheadings (H3), less prominent borders, some secondary text, subtle background accents.
  • Golden Ochre (#D4A017): Links on hover/focus, important highlights or icons, call-to-action button text (if contrast allows against primary), or as a distinct highlight color for warnings/notes.
  • Light Beige (#F5F5DC): Main content background area for optimal readability.
  • Dark Grey (#333333): General paragraph text, list items, and other body content.
